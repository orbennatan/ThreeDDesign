Tell the ironsmith this as a **fabrication requirement**, not as a casual request:

```text
Please fabricate as a structural welded stair frame, not decorative welding.

All stair stringers, tread supports, podest frames, posts, truss web members, top chords, and deck connection points must be continuously welded where they meet.

Use full-perimeter fillet welds around RHS tube joints wherever accessible.

For 3 mm wall RHS members, use approximately 3 mm fillet welds.
For 4 mm wall RHS members, use approximately 4 mm fillet welds.
Do not burn through the tube walls.

Grind only sharp edges and dangerous projections. Do not grind structural welds flat unless needed locally and agreed.

All welds should be continuous, visually uniform, with no cracks, porosity, undercut, lack of fusion, or missed corners.

Close/seal open tube ends with welded caps where water can enter.

After welding, clean welds, remove slag/spatter, prime all bare steel and weld zones, and apply corrosion protection suitable for outdoor use.

The stair must be welded to the deck frame at the upper podest, and the lower podest posts must be welded/anchored to base plates fixed to concrete/solid support.

Please confirm:
- welding method: MIG/MAG or stick
- electrode/wire type
- fillet weld size
- whether welds are continuous or intermittent
- base plate thickness
- anchor bolt size/type
- corrosion protection method
```

Important: do **not** tell him “the calculation proves it is safe.” Tell him the calculation assumes proper structural welds, full contact, good anchors, and no weak/missed joints. A bad weld or bad anchor can invalidate the whole design.
